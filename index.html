The Mummer's Dance

Hello and welcome to my Tarot Stories page. As a tribute to artist Pamela Colman Smith, I have written short stories based on a few of the tarot cards she designed.

Arthur Edward Waite (2 October 1857 – 19 May 1942), an American-born British poet and scholarly mystic, comissioned a deck to Smith upon meeting her at the Hermetic Order of the Golden Dawn, where they studied several esoteric topics including cartomancy.

Together, they were responsible for popularizing the tarot deck and making it available to mass production and distribution. Until then, it was an exclusive, hand-made and extremely expensive object, reserved only for a few people

According to Smith, one could figure out the message behind any card by simply staring at it, drawing its meaning - either from memory or the subconscious.
